YUI

"Reconnecting Through the Exploration of Dance" In a world that seems to overwhelm us with information, the importance of presence and our connection to the natural world often slips away from us. As British sculptor and environmentalist Andy Goldsworthy beautifully puts it, "We often forget that we are nature. Nature is not something separate from us. So when we say we have lost our connection to nature, we've lost our connection to ourselves."

The dance film YUI seeks to rekindle these forgotten connections, weaving together the intricate threads that bind us to our inner selves, to the beautiful bonds we share with one another, including nature.

YUI: Okinawan dialect word that represents the idea of people coming together, forming bonds, and sharing experiences. Okinawa is known for its strong sense of community and social bonds, and often values the concept of “yui” as a culture.

I hope that this short film will serve as a reminder that these connections are essential threads in our human experience.

『ダンスを通じて再びつながる 』

常に忙しい社会の中で、多くの情報を得ながら生活をしている私達。社会の流れに生きる中での大事な『繋がり』を少しづつ見失っていっているのではないのかと思ってしまう。イギリスの彫刻家であり環境保護活動家でもあるアンディゴールズワージーは、こう言っている。私たちはよく、私達自身が自然そのものであることを忘れてしまいます。自然は私たちから離れた存在ではありません。だから、私たちが自然とのつながりを失ったと言うとき、私たちは自分自身とのつながりを失ったと言えるでしょう。

ダンスフィルムYUIは、私たちの自分自身の繋がり、人と人との繋がり、自然との深い絆を結びつける複雑な糸を紡ぎながら、忘れ去られた「繋がり」を再び呼び覚まそうとしている。

ゆい： 沖縄の方言で、人々が集まり、絆を結び、経験を分かち合うことを表す言葉。沖縄は強い共同体意識と社会的絆で知られ、文化として「結い」の概念を大切にすることが多い。

このフィルムは、私たち自身、互い、自然界への深いつながり、を再発見するよう観客を誘い、これらのつながりが私たちの人間体験に不可欠な糸であることを思い出させてくれることを願い作成しました。

Filmed in Okinawa Japan

In 2023, I had the incredible opportunity to showcase my dance film at an event titled 'Love the Earth' in Osaka, Japan. This week-long event brought together young, passionate small business owners, each dedicated to nature and sustainability. Their unique products and artistic expressions created an inspiring environment that perfectly complemented the themes of my film.

Collective Shell Art: After viewing the film, I guided each individual to contribute a shell I collected from Okinawa, culminating in a collective art piece that showcases the power of collaboration. The final piece ended up with 120 shell pieces delicately stacked together by 120 people! 動画を観た後、沖縄で集めた貝殻を一人一人に置いてもらい、みんなが集まったときに何ができるかを示す集合的なアート作品を作ってもらいました。最終日には、120個の貝殻を120人で積み上げた結果綺麗なアートピースが出来上がりました。

About

Welcome to my portfolio, where I explore the intersection of art, culture, and sustainability. Having grown up in both San Francisco and Okinawa, Japan, I have always been deeply interested in global issues affecting both countries. Environmental issues specifically resonated with me, as nature provided a constant source of grounding while I moved back and forth between two very different cultures. During my time in nature, I witnessed firsthand the dying coral reefs on the beaches of Okinawa and the ongoing wildfires that compromised air quality in San Francisco. Alarmed by the devastating effects of climate change and motivated by my growing concern, I created my own social media platform to share and inspire others to adopt a more environmentally conscious lifestyle. Beyond the platform, I wanted to combine this interest with my 20 years of ballet experience to create pieces that touch people's hearts and deepen their understanding of our connection to the greater natural world, which ultimately led me to start making dance films. I believe that art plays an essential role in fostering an emotional connection, and through my films, I hope to share the profound connections we have with nature and create a community of viewers to take action against climate change.
